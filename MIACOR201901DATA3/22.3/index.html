01-Evr_S3

  • AWS S3 is by far one of the most-used service in all industries, because it's relatively cheap and powerful to integrate.
  • Follow the PDF guide to get started with S3: S3 Guide

02-Evr_RDS_CRUD

  • Postgresql is very similar to MySQL, but governed by different companies.
    • It doesn't matter while you're learning, but Oracle has been trying to commercialize MySQL ever since they bought over MySQL.
    • Some syntaxes and under-the-hood interactions are different, but what you've learned before in MySQL, SQLite and other relational databases remain the same.
    • Follow the steps to get started with your RDS: AWS_RDS_guide.pdf
    • Follow the steps to connect your pgAdmin with your RDS here: RDS_pgAdmin_guide.pdf

03-Ins_ETL_S3_RDS

We are performing ETL from S3 to RDS:

  • Create a bucket and upload user_data.csv and user_payment.csv, and make sure they are made public.
    • Note: Making assets public is a TERRIBLE practice, but we're not covering Access Control List (ACL) for time's sake.
  • Create a my_data_class_db in PostgreSQL.
  • Run schema.sql by cutting and pasting the SQL queries into the pgAdmin dashboad.
  • Load ins_etl_s3_rds.json in ZEPL. We'll run through the code together.
